## 1. Strategic Foundation

### Why MERIDIAN Exists

Sustainability consulting engagements fail not from lack of technical knowledge — they fail from coordination breakdowns. The GHG inventory gets done with one boundary assumption; the CDP submission uses a different one. The board deck cites a number the CFO has never seen. The sustainability report makes a "net zero by 2040" claim that hasn't been cleared by legal or substantiated by a roadmap.

MERIDIAN prevents these failures by treating a sustainability engagement as a coordinated pipeline with structured handoffs, quality gates, and a greenwashing firewall that protects every client-facing deliverable.

### The Core Principles

**1. Evidence over assertion**: Every GHG figure has a source. Every claim has substantiation. Every recommendation has a cost, an owner, and a timeline. "We believe" is not a data point.

**2. Greenwashing firewall, always**: No claim leaves the engagement without Greenwashing Auditor clearance. "We're in a hurry" is not an exception — it is precisely when greenwashing happens.

**3. Consistency across deliverables**: One source of truth for all numbers. The board deck, CDP response, CSRD report, and investor factsheet use the same GHG figures for the same year. Version drift is a liability.

**4. No dead-ends**: Every finding has a recommended action. Every recommendation has a named owner. Every deliverable has a clear next step.

**5. Audience-first communication**: Technical rigor is non-negotiable. But the board presentation should open with "$28M carbon cost exposure by 2030," not "Scope 1 emissions were 14,230 tCO2e."

## 2. The MERIDIAN Operating Model

### Engagement Structure

```
Phase 0: Intake & Scoping
    ↓ [Quality Gate 0: Scope confirmed, regulatory triggers mapped]
Phase 1: Baseline Assessment
    ↓ [Quality Gate 1: GHG inventory complete, data quality assessed]
Phase 2: Materiality
    ↓ [Quality Gate 2: IROs identified, material topics confirmed]
Phase 3: Strategy & Roadmap
    ↓ [Quality Gate 3: Targets set, roadmap costed, board-ready]
Phase 4: Disclosure & Reporting
    ↓ [Quality Gate 4: Greenwashing audit cleared, assurance-ready]
Final Deliverables
```

### Agent Interaction Model

Agents do not work in isolation. MERIDIAN defines:

- **Lead agents**: own a phase output and are accountable for quality
- **Supporting agents**: contribute specific analyses or data to the lead agent
- **Review agents**: validate outputs before quality gate clearance (Greenwashing Auditor, Data Consolidator)
- **Delivery agents**: translate technical outputs into audience-appropriate deliverables (Client Deliverable Generator)

### Escalation Rules

- If a quality gate fails, the failing phase lead documents the gap and returns to the relevant step — phases do not advance with known gaps
- If a data gap cannot be resolved, it is documented as a limitation in the disclosure with a DQS score and improvement plan
- If a greenwashing audit finding cannot be resolved, the claim is removed — it is not weakened language that still fails — and the gap is escalated to the client with options

## 3. Phase 0 — Intake & Scoping

**Lead agent**: MERIDIAN Orchestrator
**Duration**: 1–2 weeks
**Output**: Engagement scope document, regulatory trigger map, agent activation plan

### Objectives

- Understand why the client is engaging (regulatory trigger? investor pressure? voluntary leadership?)
- Map the regulatory landscape: CSRD applicability? CA SB 253/261? SEC exposure? UK TCFD?
- Identify the client's current sustainability maturity level and existing data assets
- Scope the engagement: which analyses, which frameworks, which deliverables, which audiences
- Assign the right agents for the right tasks — don't over-staff; don't leave gaps

### Key Activities

1. **Regulatory trigger assessment**: which mandatory frameworks apply (jurisdiction × company size × sector)?
2. **Voluntary commitment review**: SBTi committed? CDP signatory? RE100? TNFD adopter? TCFD reporter?
3. **Existing documentation review**: prior GHG inventories, sustainability reports, materiality assessments, ESG ratings
4. **Data asset inventory**: what data exists, what quality, what gaps, which systems (ERP, utility accounts, supply chain)?
5. **Stakeholder mapping**: who are the primary audiences for outputs? board? investors? regulators? public?
6. **Timeline and milestone planning**: regulatory deadlines drive the calendar

### Quality Gate 0

Before advancing to Phase 1, confirm:
- ☐ Regulatory trigger map complete — mandatory frameworks identified with applicable phase-in dates
- ☐ Engagement scope agreed — deliverables list, frameworks in scope, exclusions documented
- ☐ Agent activation plan confirmed — which agents, which phases, handoff sequence
- ☐ Data collection plan initiated — templates issued to client data owners
- ☐ Timeline confirmed — milestones tied to regulatory deadlines

## 4. Phase 1 — Baseline Assessment

**Lead agents**: GHG Inventory Specialist, ESG Data Analyst
**Supporting agents**: Scope 3 Analyst, Energy Efficiency Auditor, Sustainability Data Consolidator
**Duration**: 4–8 weeks (data-intensive)
**Output**: Verified GHG inventory, ESG baseline dataset, data quality assessment

### Objectives

- Establish the complete GHG emissions baseline (Scope 1, 2, 3) per GHG Protocol
- Assess data quality (DQS 1–5) for each metric and emission source
- Establish the organizational boundary and consolidation approach
- Identify data gaps, gap-fill methodologies, and improvement priorities
- Produce assurance-ready documentation

### Key Activities

1. **Boundary setting** (Sustainability Data Consolidator): organizational consolidation approach (operational/financial/equity control); entity-level inclusion/exclusion; base year selection and recalculation policy
2. **Scope 1 inventory** (GHG Inventory Specialist): stationary combustion, mobile combustion, process emissions, fugitive emissions; activity-based calculation; AR6 GWPs per IPCC
3. **Scope 2 inventory** (GHG Inventory Specialist): location-based and market-based; market instruments (PPAs, RECs, GOs) per GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance; supplier-specific EFs where available
4. **Scope 3 inventory** (Scope 3 Analyst): screen all 15 categories; quantify material categories using activity-based, spend-based, or hybrid methods; EEIO for spend-based; DQS score per category
5. **ESG data baseline** (ESG Data Analyst): energy intensity, water withdrawal and consumption, waste generation, social metrics (headcount, gender pay, safety) — all to DQS ≥3
6. **Data consolidation** (Sustainability Data Consolidator): multi-entity rollup; intercompany netting; gap-fill documentation; restatement log if applicable
7. **Assurance readiness** (ESG Data Analyst): evidence package compilation; verifier engagement assessment

### Quality Gate 1

Before advancing to Phase 2, confirm:
- ☐ GHG inventory complete for Scope 1 and 2 (both location and market-based)
- ☐ Scope 3 screening complete; material categories quantified at DQS ≥2
- ☐ Organizational boundary documented and applied consistently
- ☐ Data quality scores assigned for all reported metrics
- ☐ Gap-fill methodology documented for all estimated data
- ☐ Base year restatement policy in writing
- ☐ Master data table created — single source of truth for all KPIs

## 5. Phase 2 — Materiality

**Lead agent**: Materiality Facilitator
**Supporting agents**: CSRD Advisor, Climate Risk Analyst, Stakeholder Specialist, TNFD Strategist, Human Rights Advisor
**Duration**: 3–6 weeks
**Output**: Double materiality assessment, IRO register, material topic list, ESRS DMA

### Objectives

- Identify and assess impacts, risks, and opportunities (IROs) across sustainability topics
- Apply the ESRS double materiality methodology (impact materiality + financial materiality)
- Engage stakeholders in materiality assessment per ESRS 1 requirements
- Produce ESRS 1-compliant DMA documentation
- Identify material ESRS standards for disclosure (which standards apply; which are not material)

### Key Activities

1. **IRO universe construction** (Materiality Facilitator): map all potential impacts, risks, and opportunities across all ESRS topical standards; start with sector-specific considerations; include value chain
2. **Impact materiality scoring** (Materiality Facilitator): assess severity (scale × scope × irremediability) and likelihood for negative impacts; scale and likelihood for positive impacts; time horizon
3. **Financial materiality scoring** (Climate Risk Analyst for climate; TNFD Strategist for nature): assess risks and opportunities for potential financial effect; probability × magnitude; short/medium/long-term
4. **Stakeholder engagement** (Stakeholder Specialist): design and facilitate stakeholder consultation; document stakeholder perspectives on material topics; map stakeholders to IROs
5. **Climate risk assessment** (Climate Risk Analyst): TCFD-aligned physical and transition risk assessment using NGFS/IEA scenarios; quantified where possible; qualitative minimum
6. **Nature dependency and impact** (TNFD Strategist): LEAP assessment for material nature exposure; dependencies and impacts on ecosystem services; SBTN target-setting opportunities
7. **Human rights due diligence** (Human Rights Advisor): OECD 6-step screening; salient human rights issues; high-risk geography/sector mapping; CSDDD scope assessment
8. **DMA documentation** (CSRD Advisor): ESRS 1-compliant disclosure of the DMA process; material topic list; non-material topic rationale; ESRS application matrix

### Quality Gate 2

Before advancing to Phase 3, confirm:
- ☐ All ESRS topical standards screened — material vs. not material documented with rationale
- ☐ IRO register complete — all material IROs scored, time-horizoned, and source-mapped
- ☐ Stakeholder consultation documented — perspectives recorded and reflected in assessment
- ☐ Climate risk assessment complete — at minimum qualitative scenarios; quantified where feasible
- ☐ DMA narrative drafted per ESRS 1 requirements
- ☐ Material topics list agreed with client leadership

## 6. Phase 3 — Strategy & Roadmap

**Lead agents**: Net Zero Strategist, Sustainability Strategy Advisor
**Supporting agents**: Science-Based Targets Advisor, Carbon Markets Analyst, Renewable Energy Advisor, Circular Economy Strategist, Energy Efficiency Auditor, Nature-Based Solutions Advisor, EU Taxonomy Analyst
**Duration**: 4–8 weeks
**Output**: Net zero roadmap, SBTi submission, decarbonization investment plan, implementation tracker

### Objectives

- Set science-aligned emissions targets (SBTi near-term and net zero)
- Design the decarbonization roadmap with prioritized abatement levers and financial quantification
- Identify renewable energy strategy and carbon market role (if any)
- Develop circular economy and resource efficiency strategies for material topics
- Produce board-ready strategy documents with financial framing

### Key Activities

1. **Target scope and ambition** (Science-Based Targets Advisor): determine Scope 1+2 reduction target (absolute or intensity; 42% by 2030 for 1.5°C); Scope 3 target (if >40% of total); FLAG targets if relevant; long-term net zero commitment (Scope 1+2+3, ≥90% reduction by 2050)
2. **Abatement lever analysis** (Net Zero Strategist): MACC analysis across Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3 categories; cost-effectiveness ranking; technology readiness; capex/opex estimates
3. **Renewable energy pathway** (Renewable Energy Advisor): PPA vs. REC vs. on-site generation analysis; 24/7 CFE pathway if applicable; RE100 commitment assessment; Scope 2 market-based trajectory
4. **Circular economy strategy** (Circular Economy Strategist): apply 10-R hierarchy to material topics; design-for-circularity opportunities; supply chain material flow mapping; business model circularity
5. **Nature targets** (TNFD Strategist, Nature-Based Solutions Advisor): SBTN target-setting; NbS for residual emissions; biodiversity net positive pathway; EUDR compliance for forest-risk commodities
6. **Carbon market strategy** (Carbon Markets Analyst): assess role of carbon credits in residual emissions strategy; VCMI claims pathway; credit quality standards (ICVCM CCPs); avoid using credits to substitute for reductions
7. **EU Taxonomy alignment** (EU Taxonomy Analyst): screen activities against eligible activities; assess substantial contribution; DNSH across all 6 environmental objectives; MSS verification; Article 8 KPI projection
8. **Financial modelling** (Sustainability Strategy Advisor): investment required; cost of carbon exposure; risk reduction value; NPV of abatement investments; comparison to cost of inaction; board-ready financial framing

### Quality Gate 3

Before advancing to Phase 4, confirm:
- ☐ SBTi near-term target scope and ambition confirmed — ready for submission or commitment
- ☐ Decarbonization roadmap complete — lever-by-lever, costed, with interim milestones
- ☐ Renewable energy strategy agreed — Scope 2 market-based trajectory defined
- ☐ Carbon market strategy documented — role of credits, quality standards, VCMI claims pathway
- ☐ Financial summary complete — investment, risk reduction, NPV; board-presentable
- ☐ Board briefing deck drafted (by Client Deliverable Generator — board version)

## 7. Phase 4 — Disclosure & Reporting

**Lead agents**: ESG Report Writer, Client Deliverable Generator
**Supporting agents**: CDP Specialist, CSRD Advisor, Materiality Facilitator, Greenwashing Auditor, Sustainability Data Consolidator
**Duration**: 4–8 weeks
**Output**: CSRD/GRI/ISSB report, CDP submission, board deliverables, investor ESG factsheet — all greenwashing-cleared

### Objectives

- Translate the engagement's technical work into disclosure-ready outputs
- Produce all required regulatory and voluntary framework disclosures
- Ensure every external claim passes Greenwashing Auditor review
- Deliver audience-specific versions of key findings for all stakeholder groups
- Prepare for third-party assurance

### Key Activities

1. **Source of truth confirmation** (Sustainability Data Consolidator): lock the master data table — all KPIs, all figures, all methodology notes; all subsequent deliverables draw from this single source
2. **CSRD/GRI report drafting** (ESG Report Writer): narrative disclosures for all material ESRS topics; data tables per GRI Standards; CEO letter; performance highlights; case studies; data appendix with methodology
3. **CDP submission** (CDP Specialist): complete questionnaire for Climate, Water, and/or Forests as applicable; C0–C15 climate modules; A-list scoring strategy; evidence attachment
4. **Audience-specific deliverables** (Client Deliverable Generator):
   - Board briefing deck (10–15 slides; financial framing; decision and ask)
   - Executive summary (2 pages; key findings; recommended actions)
   - CFO/finance annex (quantified risk table; cost-benefit; accounting treatment)
   - Investor ESG factsheet (ISSB-aligned; year-over-year; methodology disclosed)
   - Legal disclosure language (appropriately hedged; SEC-consistent; forward-looking qualifications)
5. **Greenwashing audit** (Greenwashing Auditor): full claim audit matrix for every external document; FTC/EU/ISO/VCMI check; PASS/CONDITIONAL PASS/FAIL rating per claim; revised language for all fails
6. **Regulatory submission review** (CSRD Advisor, SEC Climate Expert): XBRL tagging for CSRD; SEC consistency check for US reporters; CA SB 253/261 compliance check
7. **Assurance package** (ESG Data Analyst, Sustainability Data Consolidator): evidence package by metric and entity; management assertion letter; verifier engagement; ISAE 3000 limited or reasonable assurance pathway

### Quality Gate 4

Before finalizing, confirm:
- ☐ Master data table locked — all deliverables use consistent numbers
- ☐ Greenwashing audit complete — PASS or CONDITIONAL PASS (with revisions applied) for all external claims
- ☐ All regulatory submissions complete and cross-referenced
- ☐ All audience-specific deliverables produced and approved by relevant internal stakeholders
- ☐ Assurance evidence package complete (if assurance is in scope)
- ☐ Internal consistency check: GHG figures match across all deliverables for same reporting year
- ☐ Legal and communications teams have reviewed and approved external-facing language

## 9. Quality Gates

### Gate Criteria Summary

Every phase gate requires documented evidence — not assertions. "We think this is complete" does not clear a gate. "Here is the evidence" does.

| Gate | Phase Complete When... |
|---|---|
| Gate 0 | Scope agreed in writing; regulatory triggers mapped; agent plan confirmed; data collection initiated |
| Gate 1 | GHG inventory verified internally; DQS scores assigned; master data table created; gap-fill documented |
| Gate 2 | IRO register complete; DMA narrative drafted; material topics agreed by client; stakeholder consultation documented |
| Gate 3 | Targets defined and costed; roadmap milestones confirmed; financial summary board-ready; board deck drafted |
| Gate 4 | Greenwashing audit cleared for all external deliverables; internal consistency confirmed; assurance package complete |

### Failure Protocol

If a gate cannot be cleared:
1. The failing lead agent documents: what is missing, why, what is needed to close the gap
2. MERIDIAN Orchestrator notifies the client of the gap and timeline to resolution
3. Phase does not advance until the gate criteria are met
4. If a gap cannot be resolved within the engagement scope, it is disclosed as a limitation with a DQS score and a next-year improvement plan

## 10. Handoff Protocols

### Standard Handoff Package

Every agent-to-agent handoff includes:

```markdown
## Handoff: [From Agent] → [To Agent]
**Date**: [Date]
**Phase**: [Phase number and name]
**Deliverables transferred**: [List of files/documents/data tables]

### Key Findings Summary
[3–5 bullet points: the most important things the receiving agent needs to know]

### Data Handed Off
| Metric | Value | DQS | Source | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Key metric] | [Value] | [1–5] | [Source] | [Any limitations] |

### Open Items
[Anything unresolved that the receiving agent needs to address]

### What NOT to Change
[Any numbers, boundaries, or decisions that are locked — do not reopen without explicit authorization]
```

### Critical Handoff Rules

- **Never start cold**: receiving agent must review the handoff package before beginning work
- **Don't relitigate locked decisions**: if the GHG Protocol consolidation approach was decided in Phase 1 with the client, it is locked — don't reopen it in Phase 4
- **Flag inconsistencies immediately**: if a receiving agent finds a number that doesn't match prior work, stop and resolve before proceeding
- **Master data table is the arbiter**: if a number in a handoff package conflicts with the master data table, the master data table governs

## 11. The Greenwashing Firewall

### Non-Negotiable Rule

**No client-facing document with an environmental claim leaves Phase 4 without Greenwashing Auditor clearance.**

This rule has no exceptions. "We're in a hurry" is precisely when greenwashing happens. The Greenwashing Auditor is not a bottleneck — it is the mechanism that protects the client from regulatory enforcement, investor litigation, and reputational damage.

### What Requires Greenwashing Audit

- All sustainability reports (GRI, ISSB, integrated)
- All CDP submissions
- All investor ESG communications
- All board and executive presentations with sustainability claims
- All press releases, website copy, product labels with environmental claims
- All regulatory submissions (CSRD, SEC, UK TCFD)

### What Does Not Require Greenwashing Audit

- Internal data workbooks (for internal use only, clearly marked)
- Methodology documentation (no public claims)
- Raw data tables without interpretive claims

### Audit Standards Applied

The Greenwashing Auditor applies, as applicable:
- FTC Green Guides (16 CFR Part 260) — US market claims
- EU Green Claims Directive (proposed) — EU market claims
- EU Empowering Consumers Directive 2024/825/EU — EU commercial practices
- UK CMA Green Claims Code (2021) — UK market claims
- ISO 14021:2016 — Self-declared product claims
- VCMI Claims Code of Practice (2023) — Carbon credit claims
- ISO 14068-1:2023 — Carbon neutrality claims
- SEC Rule 10b-5 / climate disclosure consistency — US securities exposure

## 13. Risk Management

### Common Engagement Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client data gaps delay Phase 1 | High | Medium | Issue data templates at Phase 0; set hard deadlines with buffer |
| Scope 3 Category 11 data unavailable | High | Medium | Apply spend-based EEIO as interim; document gap; DQS 2; improvement plan |
| Board rejects strategy recommendation | Medium | High | Pre-align with CFO/CSO before board presentation; present financial framing first |
| Greenwashing audit fails a major claim | Medium | High | Audit early in Phase 4; don't wait for final draft; provide alternative language immediately |
| Base year restatement required post-SBTi submission | Low | High | Lock base year before SBTi submission; set recalculation policy in Phase 1 |
| Regulatory framework changes during engagement | Medium | Medium | Monitor regulatory calendar; flag changes to client; build flexibility into target structure |
| Client communication team weakens hedging language | High | High | Greenwashing Auditor has final authority; document the original finding and the revision requested |

### Greenwashing-Specific Risk Escalation

If a client instructs the team to publish a claim that has failed the Greenwashing Audit:
1. Document the finding in writing
2. Provide the specific standard and regulation it fails
3. Offer alternative approved language
4. If client insists on the failing language: document that the claim was published against Greenwashing Auditor recommendation and the specific risk (FTC/EU/SEC)
5. Do not assist in strengthening a failing claim — only in finding a passing alternative

## 14. Success Metrics

### Engagement-Level Success

- All Phase quality gates cleared with documented evidence (not assertions)
- Greenwashing Auditor clearance achieved for all external deliverables — zero post-publication claim challenges
- GHG inventory numbers consistent across all deliverables for the same reporting year
- Board approves sustainability strategy recommendation in first presentation — not deferred
- CDP score improves year-over-year with no adverse comments on disclosure quality

### Client-Level Success

- CSRD first-year disclosure submitted on time with limited assurance clearance
- SBTi near-term target validated within 24 months of commitment
- Investor ESG queries decline or focus on forward commitments rather than disclosure gaps
- Internal teams describe deliverables as "clear," "useful," and "aligned with what we need to act" — not "technical" or "overwhelming"

### Long-Term Success Indicators

- Base year restatement policy in writing before first M&A event
- Data quality scores improve year-over-year: ≥80% of Scope 1+2 at DQS ≥4 within 2 years
- Greenwashing audit catches all claim failures before publication — zero post-publication corrections required
- Stakeholder engagement built into annual process — not a one-time materiality exercise
