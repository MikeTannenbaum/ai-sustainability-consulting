# Runbook: Net Zero Roadmap Engagement

> **Scenario**: A company has made or is planning to make a net zero commitment — or has been asked by the board, investors, or regulators to show a credible path to net zero. This engagement produces: a validated SBTi near-term target, a costed decarbonization roadmap, a renewable energy strategy, a carbon market role assessment, and a board-ready strategy presentation. From commitment to validated target in 12–18 months.

---

## When to Use This Runbook

- Company is committing to SBTi (or needs to act on an existing commitment)
- Board or investors have asked for a "credible net zero plan"
- CSRD first report requires a climate transition plan (ESRS E1-1)
- CDP submission needs quantified targets and scenario-based strategy (for A-list)
- Company is setting sustainability strategy for the first time (materiality-to-strategy)

**Prerequisite**: A defensible GHG inventory (Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3) must exist before this engagement starts. If it doesn't, run the GHG Inventory runbook first.

## Week-by-Week Timeline

### Week 1: Intake, Data Review, and Target Framing

**MERIDIAN Orchestrator**:
- Review existing GHG inventory (hand-off from inventory engagement or existing data)
- Confirm base year, consolidation approach, Scope 3 coverage
- Identify SBTi status: not committed / committed (when?) / validated
- Map regulatory requirements: CSRD E1-1 climate transition plan? CDP C4?
- Identify board/investor constraints: budget envelope? timeline preferences?
- Brief the Science-Based Targets Advisor on starting conditions

### Weeks 1–4: Target Scope and Ambition

**Science-Based Targets Advisor**:
- Determine applicable SBTi methodology: Corporate Standard vs. sector-specific (power, oil & gas, buildings, cement, steel, aluminum, pulp & paper, chemicals, maritime, aviation)
- Calculate required Scope 1+2 reduction: 42% absolute by 2030 (or 2032 for some sector pathways) from base year
- Assess Scope 3 target requirement: is Scope 3 ≥40% of total? What categories are in target scope?
- FLAG assessment: does the company have significant land-based emissions requiring FLAG targets?
- SBTN assessment: are nature-related targets appropriate to link to the strategy?
- Determine base year: is current base year appropriate? Does it pre-date major structural changes?
- Draft target formulation for SBTi submission or internal commitment

**Deliverable**: Target formulation document; SBTi method selection; coverage and base year analysis

### Weeks 3–8: Abatement Lever Analysis (MACC)

**Net Zero Strategist**:
- Map all decarbonization levers available across Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3
- For each lever: abatement potential (tCO2e/yr), implementation cost (capex + opex), net $/tCO2e, timeline, technology readiness
- Build MACC: rank levers by cost-effectiveness; identify cost-saving wins; identify economically attractive investments; identify high-cost/long-timeline residual levers
- Calculate the "gap" after all economic levers: how much remains that requires carbon markets or long-term R&D
- Identify policy dependencies: which levers require regulatory changes, grid decarbonization, or technology maturation?
- Develop 5-year milestone plan: what does the trajectory to 2030 look like lever-by-lever?

**Renewable Energy Advisor (concurrent)**:
- Scope the renewable electricity opportunity: total electricity consumption, Scope 2 current performance, procurement markets
- Assess options: on-site generation, physical PPA, virtual PPA, green tariffs, unbundled RECs/GOs
- GHG Protocol compliance check: instruments must be market-appropriate, vintage-matched
- Develop Scope 2 market-based trajectory: 2025 → 2030 → RE100 (if applicable)
- 24/7 CFE assessment if company is considering Google-style hourly matching

**Circular Economy Strategist (concurrent)**:
- Apply 10-R hierarchy to material topics from Phase 2
- Quantify circularity-related emission reductions in material Scope 3 categories (Cat 1 through design changes; Cat 12 through EoL design)
- Circular business model assessment: any product-as-a-service opportunities?
- Material Circularity Indicator baseline and improvement target

**Deliverable**: MACC analysis workbook; decarbonization trajectory to target year; renewable energy strategy; circular economy lever assessment

### Weeks 6–9: Carbon Markets and Nature Strategy

**Carbon Markets Analyst**:
- Quantify residual emissions post-abatement: how many tCO2e/yr by 2030? By 2040? By 2050?
- Assess role of carbon credits: only for residual after maximum reductions; quality standards required
- VCMI claims pathway: does company want to make VCMI claims (Silver/Gold/Platinum)? Requirements?
- Credit type strategy: favored project types; avoidance vs. removal; geographic preference
- Carbon cost exposure: EU ETS, UK ETS, California Cap-and-Trade, CBAM — quantify current and projected exposure
- Internal carbon price recommendation: shadow price for capex decisions; fee-and-dividend for behavioral change

**Nature-Based Solutions Advisor (if NbS are in strategy)**:
- Identify credible NbS opportunities: reforestation (ARR), improved forest management (IFM), soil carbon, blue carbon
- Apply IUCN Global Standard screen: does each opportunity meet NbS criteria?
- Carbon credit quality: Verra VCS + CCB methodology assessment; vintage; permanence
- Avoid: using NbS credits to substitute for operational reductions; NbS without community benefit

**Deliverable**: Carbon market strategy; VCMI claims pathway (if applicable); residual emissions plan; carbon cost exposure analysis

### Weeks 7–10: Financial Modelling

**Sustainability Strategy Advisor**:
- Compile all lever costs from MACC analysis
- Build investment timeline: capex by year (2025–2030); opex/savings by year
- Quantify risk reduction: carbon cost exposure reduction; regulatory compliance cost reduction; stranded asset risk reduction
- NPV analysis: 7% discount rate (or company's own hurdle rate); compare investment to cost of inaction
- Financing options: sustainability-linked bonds, green bonds, IRA tax credits (US), CEF (EU)
- Sensitivity analysis: what if carbon price is $100/tCO2e vs. $50? What if grid decarbonizes faster?

**Deliverable**: Financial model; NPV analysis; risk reduction quantification; board-ready financial summary

### Weeks 10–12: Deliverables and Audit

**Client Deliverable Generator**:

For Board:
- Strategy briefing deck (12 slides): why now; where we stand; what we found; the strategy; investment; roadmap; ask
- Financial summary (2-page annex): investment, risk reduction, NPV, cost of inaction
- Board resolution language (if needed): wording to formally adopt SBTi commitment or net zero strategy

For CSO / Sustainability Team:
- Full roadmap document: MACC; lever-by-lever plan; renewable energy strategy; carbon markets; circular economy; targets and milestones; implementation owners
- SBTi submission package: target formulation; base year; methodology notes; coverage rationale

For CFO / Finance:
- Carbon cost exposure analysis: EU ETS, CA, UK by year; sensitivity scenarios
- Capex/opex timeline: investment schedule; cash flow impact

For Investors:
- Net zero strategy one-pager: SBTi status; near-term target; key levers; milestones; governance

**Greenwashing Auditor**:
- Audit all claims in all external documents:
  - "Net zero by [year]": scope? reduction requirement? residual plan? SBTi validation status?
  - "Science-aligned": which science? which body? validated or committed?
  - "Carbon neutral": ISO 14068 compliance? scope definition? verification?
  - "Committed to [target]": formal commitment document? governance accountability?
- Issue PASS/CONDITIONAL PASS/FAIL per claim
- Required hedging language for forward-looking statements

**Deliverable**: Board strategy deck; full roadmap document; SBTi submission package; financial summary; greenwashing audit report; all external deliverables greenwashing-cleared

## SBTi Target Submission Checklist

Before submitting to SBTi:
- ☐ Base year GHG data: Scope 1+2 at minimum; Scope 3 if target covers S3 (required if S3 ≥40%)
- ☐ Base year recalculation policy documented
- ☐ Target coverage: % of Scope 3 categories in near-term target ≥67% (of total Scope 3) if S3 target required
- ☐ Target ambition: 42%+ absolute Scope 1+2 reduction by 2030 (or sector pathway equivalent)
- ☐ Methodology selected: Cross-sector; or sector-specific pathway if applicable
- ☐ FLAG assessment complete: FLAG targets set if FLAG emissions are significant
- ☐ Commitment letter signed by CEO or equivalent
- ☐ SBTi online submission form completed with all required fields
- ☐ Validation fee paid
- ☐ Internal communications: SBTi commitment press release reviewed by Greenwashing Auditor before publication
