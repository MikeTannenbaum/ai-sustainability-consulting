# Runbook: CSRD First-Year Disclosure Engagement

> **Scenario**: A company is within scope of the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive and needs to produce its first ESRS-aligned sustainability statement — complete with double materiality assessment, all material ESRS disclosures, XBRL tagging, and limited assurance. This is the most complex sustainability reporting engagement most companies will ever undertake. The first year determines the quality baseline for every year that follows.

---

## When to Use This Runbook

- Company is in CSRD Phase 1 (FY2024 report — largest EU-listed companies)
- Company is in CSRD Phase 2 (FY2025 report — other large EU companies and subsidiaries of non-EU groups)
- Company is in CSRD Phase 3 (FY2026 — listed SMEs; FY2028 — non-EU companies with EU nexus)
- Company is voluntarily preparing for CSRD ahead of mandatory deadline
- Company needs to assess CSRD scope and gap vs. current reporting

**CSRD scope reminder**:
- Phase 1: EU-listed companies previously subject to NFRD (>500 employees)
- Phase 2: Other large EU companies (>250 employees AND >€40M revenue OR >€20M total assets)
- Phase 3: EU-listed SMEs (<250 employees — opt-out possible until 2028)
- Phase 4: Non-EU companies with EU revenue >€150M and EU branch/subsidiary

## Phase Breakdown

### Phase A (Weeks 1–4): CSRD Scoping and Gap Assessment

**MERIDIAN Orchestrator + CSRD Advisor**:
1. Confirm CSRD applicability: phase-in date, reporting entity definition, first reporting year
2. Define CSRD reporting boundary: accounting directive boundary (may differ from GHG Protocol boundary — document both)
3. Assess gap vs. current reporting: which ESRS topics does current reporting cover? What is new?
4. Identify quick wins: what data already exists? What can be lifted from prior CDP/GRI/TCFD reports?
5. Create CSRD preparation project plan: workstreams, owners, deadlines
6. Assess XBRL tool: Workiva, Certent, IBM OpenPages, Taxonomy Tool — select and onboard

**Deliverable**: CSRD scope confirmation; gap assessment vs. current reporting; preparation project plan; XBRL tool selected

### Phase B (Weeks 3–8): Double Materiality Assessment

This is the foundation of the entire CSRD report. Everything else depends on getting this right.

**Materiality Facilitator (lead) + CSRD Advisor, Climate Risk Analyst, Stakeholder Specialist, Human Rights Advisor, TNFD Strategist**:

1. Construct the IRO universe: all potential impacts, risks, and opportunities across all ESRS topical standards
2. Screen each IRO: impact materiality (severity × likelihood) and financial materiality (probability × magnitude)
3. Stakeholder consultation: map affected stakeholders + information users; design engagement; document perspectives
4. Climate risk assessment (Climate Risk Analyst): NGFS scenario analysis; physical risk quantification; transition risk quantification; TCFD-aligned
5. Nature assessment (TNFD Strategist): LEAP screening; GBF Target 15 alignment
6. Human rights salient issues (Human Rights Advisor): OECD 6-step screening; CSDDD scope assessment
7. Determine material topics: score IROs; apply thresholds; produce material topic list
8. Complete DMA documentation per ESRS 1: process description; stakeholder consultation summary; thresholds applied; material topic list; non-material topic rationale

**ESRS application matrix**:

For each material standard: identify which specific disclosure requirements apply. For each non-material standard: document the rationale (this goes in the sustainability statement per ESRS 2 IRO-2).

**Deliverable**: IRO register; DMA narrative; ESRS application matrix; stakeholder consultation record; climate risk assessment; nature screening; human rights salient issues

### Phase C (Weeks 2–12): Data Collection and Quantification

Multiple tracks running in parallel:

**GHG Inventory Specialist + ESG Data Analyst**:
- GHG inventory: Scope 1+2+3 (use GHG Inventory runbook for detail)
- Energy data: ESRS E1-5 energy consumption and mix (absolute + % renewable)
- All quantitative ESG metrics per material ESRS standards
- Assurance-readiness for all metrics from Day 1

**EU Taxonomy Analyst**:
- Screen all economic activities against EU Taxonomy eligible activities (Climate Delegated Act + Environmental Delegated Act)
- For eligible activities: assess against technical screening criteria (TSC) for substantial contribution to at least one of 6 objectives
- DNSH assessment for all 6 environmental objectives
- Minimum social safeguards (MSS) verification (OECD MNE Guidelines, UNGPs, ILO core conventions)
- Calculate Article 8 KPIs: turnover %, capex %, opex % — eligible and aligned
- Document activity-level evidence for verifier

**CSDDD Advisor (Human Rights Advisor)**:
- Assess CSDDD applicability (if not already done)
- If in scope: initiate HRDD program documentation for ESRS S1–S4 disclosures

**Deliverable**: Complete data set for all material ESRS standards; EU Taxonomy KPI calculation; all metrics at DQS ≥3

### Phase D (Weeks 10–16): ESRS Report Drafting

**ESG Report Writer (lead) + CSRD Advisor**:

Draft all ESRS disclosures per the application matrix:

**ESRS 2 (mandatory — all companies)**:
- GOV-1 through GOV-5: Governance of sustainability matters; board composition; management bodies; incentives; due diligence; risk management
- SBM-1 through SBM-3: Strategy; stakeholder interests; material IROs and strategy interaction
- IRO-1 through IRO-2: DMA process; ESRS application matrix (all material standards; all non-material with rationale)

**ESRS E1 (Climate — likely material for most companies)**:
- E1-1: Transition plan for climate change mitigation (if targets set; else disclose no plan or plan in development)
- E1-2: Climate policies (climate policy, energy policy, targets)
- E1-3: Actions and resources (programs, initiatives, budgets)
- E1-4: Targets (SBTi near-term; net zero; Scope 2 RE100 if applicable)
- E1-5: Energy consumption and mix (absolute; intensity; % renewable — both location and market)
- E1-6: GHG emissions (Scope 1; Scope 2 location + market; Scope 3 by category; total; intensity)
- E1-7: Removals and carbon credits (if applicable)
- E1-8: Internal carbon pricing (if applicable)
- E1-9: Financial effects from climate risks and opportunities

**[Continue for all material ESRS standards per application matrix]**

**Drafting principles**:
- Data first: present the number, then the context — don't bury metrics in narrative
- No forward commitments without substantiation: ESRS requires disclosure of policies, actions, AND targets — if a target isn't set, say "target to be established by [date]" not silence
- Connectivity: ESRS 1 requires connectivity of information — show how material topics connect to strategy and financial position
- Assurance alignment: every disclosed figure must trace to source data in the evidence package

**Deliverable**: Full draft ESRS sustainability statement; all material topics disclosed per application matrix; EU Taxonomy Article 8 KPIs integrated

### Phase E (Weeks 14–18): XBRL Tagging

**ESG Data Analyst**:
- Apply EFRAG ESRS XBRL taxonomy to all mandatory quantitative disclosures
- Block tagging for narrative disclosures (phase 1 requirement — element tagging in later years)
- File format: Inline XBRL (iXBRL) embedded in HTML sustainability statement
- Review taxonomy extensions required for any non-standard disclosures
- Technical validation: run XBRL validation before submission
- Cross-check: tagged values must match reported values — no discrepancies

**Deliverable**: XBRL-tagged sustainability statement; validation report

### Phase F (Weeks 15–20): Assurance and Finalization

**ESG Data Analyst + Sustainability Data Consolidator**:
- Finalize evidence package: all reported metrics with source documentation
- Prepare management assertion letter
- Brief external assurance provider (limited assurance per ISAE 3000 for Year 1)
- Address verifier queries; provide additional evidence as requested
- Incorporate verifier comments into final report

**Client Deliverable Generator**:
- Board summary: 3-page brief on CSRD first report; material findings; what the disclosure commits the company to
- Investor ESG summary: key metrics; year-over-year where available; target status
- Internal training: summary of material topics and what they mean for each business unit

**Greenwashing Auditor**:
- Full claim audit of sustainability statement: every environmental claim checked against FTC/EU standards
- EU Green Claims Directive and Empowering Consumers Directive compliance check (EU market context)
- Specific attention: carbon neutrality claims; net zero language; product sustainability claims embedded in report
- Issue PASS/CONDITIONAL PASS/FAIL per claim; provide revised language for all fails

**Deliverable**: Assurance-ready sustainability statement; verifier engagement complete; board summary; greenwashing audit cleared; XBRL file submitted

## CSRD/ESRS Compliance Checklist

**General (ESRS 2)**:
- ☐ GOV-1: Board sustainability governance disclosed
- ☐ GOV-3: Sustainability-linked incentives disclosed (or confirmed not applicable)
- ☐ GOV-4: Due diligence statement included
- ☐ SBM-1: Business model and sustainability strategy described
- ☐ SBM-3: All material IROs disclosed with strategy interaction
- ☐ IRO-1: DMA process described
- ☐ IRO-2: ESRS application matrix included (all standards — material and non-material with rationale)

**Climate (ESRS E1)**:
- ☐ E1-1: Transition plan (or confirmed not yet developed — with expected date)
- ☐ E1-5: Energy consumption absolute + intensity + % renewable (both methods)
- ☐ E1-6: Scope 1, 2 (location + market), Scope 3 by category, total, GHG intensity
- ☐ E1-9: Anticipated financial effects from climate risks (at minimum qualitative with quantification where feasible)

**XBRL**:
- ☐ All mandatory quantitative disclosures tagged
- ☐ Narrative blocks tagged
- ☐ XBRL validation passed — no errors
- ☐ iXBRL file embedded in HTML sustainability statement

**Assurance**:
- ☐ Limited assurance engagement completed (ISAE 3000)
- ☐ Assurance statement included in sustainability statement
- ☐ No adverse findings from assurance provider (or findings addressed)
