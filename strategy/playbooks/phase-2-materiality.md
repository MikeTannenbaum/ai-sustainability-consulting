# Phase 2: Materiality Playbook

> **MERIDIAN Phase 2** — The assessment that determines what the company discloses, what targets it sets, and what the board needs to govern. Get the IROs right here and the strategy will be grounded in what actually matters. Skip this, and the disclosure will be generic and the strategy will miss the issues that matter to investors, regulators, and affected communities.

---

## Phase Overview

**Lead Agent**: Materiality Facilitator
**Supporting Agents**: CSRD Advisor, Climate Risk Analyst, Stakeholder Specialist, TNFD Strategist, Human Rights Advisor
**Duration**: 3–6 weeks
**Primary output**: Double materiality assessment + IRO register + material topic list + DMA documentation

---

## Step 1: IRO Universe Construction

**Lead**: Materiality Facilitator

### What is an IRO?

- **Impact**: the company's actual or potential effect on people and the environment — positive or negative, actual or potential, short/medium/long-term, through own operations or value chain
- **Risk**: sustainability-related financial risks that could affect the company's financial position, performance, cash flows, access to finance, or cost of capital
- **Opportunity**: sustainability-related opportunities that could benefit the company financially

### Constructing the universe

Start with ESRS standards as the structure. Every ESRS topical standard represents a potential topic area:

**Environment**
- ESRS E1: Climate change (mitigation, adaptation, energy)
- ESRS E2: Pollution (air, water, soil, substances of concern, microplastics)
- ESRS E3: Water and marine resources
- ESRS E4: Biodiversity and ecosystems
- ESRS E5: Resource use and circular economy

**Social**
- ESRS S1: Own workforce (working conditions, equal treatment, other rights)
- ESRS S2: Workers in value chain
- ESRS S3: Affected communities
- ESRS S4: Consumers and end-users

**Governance**
- ESRS G1: Business conduct (corporate culture, whistleblowing, anti-corruption, lobbying)

For each standard: identify the specific IROs relevant to this company's sector, value chain, and geography. Do not use the ESRS standard topics as a template to fill in — identify actual IROs specific to this company.

### Sector-specific IRO addition

Layer on sector-specific IROs from:
- SASB industry standards (sector-specific material issues)
- GRI sector standards (sector-specific disclosures)
- Investor engagement letters (what investors are asking about in this sector)
- Peer company disclosures (what material topics appear across the sector)
- Regulatory enforcement patterns (what issues regulators are focused on)

## Step 2: Impact Materiality Assessment

**Lead**: Materiality Facilitator

### ESRS 1 impact materiality criteria

An impact is material if it is actual or potential, and significant based on:

**For negative impacts**:
- **Severity**: the combination of (a) scale — how grave the impact, (b) scope — how many people/how much environment affected, (c) irremediability — how hard to remediate
- **Likelihood**: for potential impacts — the probability that the impact will occur

**For positive impacts**:
- **Scale**: the magnitude of the benefit to people or environment
- **Scope**: how many people or how much environment benefits
- **Likelihood**: for potential impacts

### Scoring approach

Use a 1–5 scale for each dimension:

| Score | Scale/Scope | Irremediability | Likelihood |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | Catastrophic / Global | Irreversible | Near-certain |
| 4 | Severe / Regional | Very difficult to remediate | Likely |
| 3 | Significant / Multi-country | Difficult to remediate | Moderate |
| 2 | Moderate / Local | Some remediation possible | Unlikely |
| 1 | Minor / Site-level | Fully remediable | Remote |

Severity score = Max(Scale, Scope) × Irremediability factor (or weighted combination per company policy)

Overall impact materiality score = Severity × Likelihood (for potential) or Severity alone (for actual)

### Time horizons

Apply ESRS 1 time horizons consistently:
- **Short-term**: current reporting period and up to 1 year
- **Medium-term**: 1–5 years
- **Long-term**: beyond 5 years

## Step 3: Financial Materiality Assessment

**Lead**: Climate Risk Analyst (for climate); TNFD Strategist (for nature); Materiality Facilitator (overall)

### ESRS 1 financial materiality criteria

A risk or opportunity is financially material if it could generate significant effects on:
- Cash flows
- Access to finance
- Cost of capital
- Financial position or performance

Assessment: probability × magnitude of potential financial effect

### Climate risk financial materiality

**Physical risks** (Climate Risk Analyst):
- Acute physical risks: extreme weather events (hurricanes, floods, wildfires, extreme heat) affecting facilities, supply chain, or customers
- Chronic physical risks: sea level rise, changing precipitation, long-term temperature shifts
- Scenarios: NGFS (Network for Greening the Financial System) — RCP2.6 (orderly), RCP4.5 (disorderly), RCP8.5 (hot house world)
- IEA scenarios: Net Zero Emissions by 2050 (NZE), Sustainable Development Scenario (SDS), Stated Policies Scenario (STEPS)

**Transition risks** (Climate Risk Analyst):
- Policy and legal: carbon pricing, emissions regulations, stranded asset risk
- Technology: disruption from clean technology, high-carbon technology obsolescence
- Market: changing customer preferences, commodity price shifts
- Reputation: stakeholder responses to GHG performance

Quantify where possible — even order-of-magnitude financial exposure ranges are more useful to the board than qualitative descriptions.

### Nature-related financial materiality

**Nature dependencies** (TNFD Strategist):
- What ecosystem services does the business depend on? (water, pollination, climate regulation, raw materials)
- Where does dependency create financial exposure if ecosystem services degrade?
- ENCORE tool: map sector activities to ecosystem service dependencies

**Nature impacts** (TNFD Strategist):
- What are the significant impacts on biodiversity and ecosystems through operations and value chain?
- IBAT screening: proximity to Key Biodiversity Areas, protected areas, critical habitats
- GBF Target 15: reporting in alignment with Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework

## Step 4: Stakeholder Engagement

**Lead**: Stakeholder Specialist

### Stakeholder mapping

Map stakeholders across two dimensions:
- **Affected stakeholders**: those materially affected by the company's impacts (workers, communities, supply chain workers, consumers)
- **Users of sustainability information**: those making decisions based on sustainability disclosures (investors, lenders, analysts, regulators, civil society)

ESRS 1 requires both types to be considered in the materiality process.

### Engagement design

| Stakeholder | Engagement Method | Materiality Input |
|---|---|---|
| Employees | Survey + focus groups | Workplace impacts, culture, working conditions |
| Supply chain workers | Desk research + NGO consultation | Labor rights, safety, living wage |
| Investors / analysts | Direct engagement + ESG questionnaire review | Financial risks, disclosure gaps |
| Customers | Survey + complaint analysis | Product safety, environmental impact of products |
| Communities | Consultation meetings or NGO proxy | Environmental impacts, community investment |
| Regulators | Regulatory filings + public positions | Compliance status, enforcement risk |
| Civil society / NGOs | Public positions + consultation | Systemic issues, reputation risk |

### FPIC considerations

For any projects or activities affecting Indigenous Peoples or local communities: Free, Prior and Informed Consent is a human rights requirement (UNGPs, IFC PS7). Document FPIC process in materiality engagement record.

## Step 5: Human Rights Due Diligence Screening

**Lead**: Human Rights Advisor

### OECD 6-step process (abbreviated for Phase 2)

1. **Embed HRDD in policy and management systems**: assess whether company policies (human rights policy, supplier code of conduct) meet UNGPs/OECD standards
2. **Identify actual and potential adverse impacts**: map value chain; identify high-risk geographies and sectors; screen against ILO core conventions
3. **Integrate findings and take action**: for salient issues identified — assess severity, prioritize for response
4. **Track implementation**: what grievance mechanisms exist? Are they accessible, trusted, effective?
5. **Communicate**: what does the company disclose about HRDD?
6. **Provide for or cooperate in remediation**: for actual impacts caused or contributed to

### CSDDD scope assessment

EU Directive 2024/1760 — assess applicability:
- >1,000 employees globally AND >€450M global net turnover → full CSDDD scope
- Non-EU companies: >€450M EU-generated net turnover → in scope
- Phase-in: largest companies from 2027; phased 2028–2029

If in scope: CSDDD requires formal HRDD program, climate transition plan, civil liability for failures.

## Step 6: DMA Documentation

**Lead**: CSRD Advisor + Materiality Facilitator

### ESRS 1 DMA documentation requirements

The disclosure of the DMA process must include:
- Overview of the DMA process and key steps
- How stakeholder perspectives were considered
- How the thresholds for material impact and financial materiality were determined and applied
- The resulting list of material sustainability topics and the ESRS standards and disclosure requirements applied
- The topics that were assessed as not material and why

### ESRS application matrix

```markdown
## ESRS Application Matrix — [Company] — FY[Year]

| ESRS Standard | Topic | Material? | Basis for Assessment | Disclosure Requirements Applied |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ESRS E1 | Climate change | Yes — Impact + Financial | Scope 1+2+3 emissions; carbon cost exposure; physical risk exposure | E1-1 through E1-9 all applicable |
| ESRS E2 | Pollution | Partial | Air emissions from combustion material; water discharge not material | E2-3 (air), E2-4 (water), E2-6 partial |
| ESRS E3 | Water | Yes — Impact | High water stress sites in manufacturing footprint | E3-1 through E3-5 |
| ESRS E4 | Biodiversity | No | No significant land-use change; no material biodiversity impacts identified | Not applied — rationale documented |
| ESRS E5 | Circular economy | Yes — Impact | Packaging waste material; product end-of-life material | E5-1, E5-4, E5-5 |
| ESRS S1 | Own workforce | Yes — Impact + Financial | 8,000 employees; health & safety material; fair wage gap identified | Full S1 disclosure |
| ESRS S2 | Value chain workers | Yes — Impact | High-risk Tier 1 suppliers in SE Asia identified | S2-1 through S2-5 |
| ESRS S3 | Affected communities | No | No material community impacts from owned operations | Not applied — rationale documented |
| ESRS S4 | Consumers | Partial | Product safety material for certain product lines | S4-3, S4-4 |
| ESRS G1 | Business conduct | Yes | Anti-corruption program required; lobbying disclosure required | G1-1 through G1-5 |
```
