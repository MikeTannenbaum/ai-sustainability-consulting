# Phase 3: Strategy & Roadmap Playbook

> **MERIDIAN Phase 3** — Where analysis turns into action. The GHG inventory and materiality assessment were the diagnosis. The strategy is the treatment plan. This phase produces the net zero roadmap, sets the science-aligned targets, costs the abatement levers, and builds the board-ready business case. The numbers have to be real — "we'll get there somehow" is not a strategy.

---

## Phase Overview

**Lead Agents**: Net Zero Strategist, Sustainability Strategy Advisor
**Supporting Agents**: Science-Based Targets Advisor, Carbon Markets Analyst, Renewable Energy Advisor, Circular Economy Strategist, Energy Efficiency Auditor, Nature-Based Solutions Advisor, EU Taxonomy Analyst
**Duration**: 4–8 weeks

## Step 1: Target Scope and Ambition Setting

**Lead**: Science-Based Targets Advisor

### SBTi Near-Term Target (required within 24 months of commitment)

**Scope 1+2 target**:
- Minimum ambition: 4.2% absolute reduction per year (= ~42% by 2030 from base year) for 1.5°C alignment
- OR intensity-based equivalent where absolute is inappropriate (SBTi approves for specific sector cases)
- Sector-specific pathways override generic methods for: power generation, oil & gas, buildings, cement, steel, aluminum, pulp & paper, chemicals

**Scope 3 target** (required if Scope 3 ≥40% of total Scope 1+2+3):
- Absolute or intensity reduction covering ≥67% of total Scope 3 emissions
- Minimum 1.5°C-aligned reduction — SBTi will prescribe minimum for sector

**FLAG (Forest, Land, Agriculture)**:
- Companies with significant land-based emissions (from FLAG sectors) must set FLAG-specific SBTi targets
- Two distinct targets: non-FLAG (fossil fuel reduction) and FLAG (land use reduction and removals)

**SBTN (Science-Based Targets for Nature)**:
- For companies with material nature dependencies or impacts: SBTN 5-step process (Assess → Interpret → Set → Act → Track)
- GBF-aligned targets for water, land, and biodiversity

### SBTi Net Zero Target (long-term)

- Scope 1+2+3: ≥90% absolute reduction by 2050 from base year (or sooner for 1.5°C pathway)
- Remaining ≤10% offset by permanent carbon removal (not avoided emissions credits)
- SBTi Corporate Net-Zero Standard validation required for net zero claims

## Step 2: Marginal Abatement Cost Curve (MACC) Analysis

**Lead**: Net Zero Strategist

### What a MACC shows

A MACC ranks decarbonization levers by:
- **Cost-effectiveness**: $/tCO2e abated (negative = cost-saving; positive = investment required)
- **Abatement potential**: how many tCO2e each lever can reduce from the baseline
- **Feasibility**: implementation timeline and technology readiness

The MACC tells you: which levers to do first (cost-saving wins), which are economically justified, and how much remains that requires carbon markets or other solutions.

### Abatement lever analysis by scope

**Scope 1 levers**:
- Fuel switching: diesel/gas → electric (EVs, heat pumps, electric forklifts)
- Efficiency improvements: combustion optimization, insulation, process efficiency
- Electrification of industrial heat: low-temperature and medium-temperature processes
- Refrigerant management: low-GWP refrigerant substitution, leak detection
- Operational optimization: route optimization, production scheduling

**Scope 2 levers**:
- Renewable electricity procurement: PPA, on-site solar, direct investment
- Energy efficiency: LED lighting, HVAC optimization, building envelope
- Behavioral and operational: load shifting, demand response, metering
- Green tariffs and market instruments: RECs/GOs as transitional measure

**Scope 3 levers (material categories)**:
- Cat 1 (Purchased goods): supplier engagement targets, low-carbon material specification, circular design
- Cat 6 (Business travel): travel policy, virtual meeting substitution, lower-carbon transport
- Cat 7 (Commuting): EV subsidy, transit subsidy, remote work policy
- Cat 11 (Product use): product redesign for energy efficiency, user behavior programs
- Cat 12 (Product EoL): extended producer responsibility, take-back programs, recyclable design

## Step 3: Renewable Energy Strategy

**Lead**: Renewable Energy Advisor

### Pathway analysis

| Option | GHG Protocol Compliance | Quality | Implementation Timeline | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| On-site solar/wind | Yes — market-based | Highest | 12–24 months | High upfront, low operating |
| Physical PPA | Yes — market-based | High (own production EF) | 18–36 months | Low variable cost; long contract |
| Virtual/Financial PPA | Yes — market-based | High (same grid, vintage-matched) | 12–24 months | Market price exposure |
| Unbundled RECs/GOs | Yes — but scrutinize | Variable (same market required) | Immediate | Low cost |
| Green tariff | Yes — market-based if compliant | Medium (utility-specific EF) | Immediate | Slight premium |

**GHG Protocol compliance check**: market instruments must be (a) from the same regional market as consumption, (b) vintage-matched to the reporting year, (c) retired on the company's behalf, (d) not double-counted.

**24/7 Carbon-Free Energy**: more ambitious than annual matching; RE100 is moving toward hourly matching; assess feasibility and timeline.

### RE100 pathway

If RE100 commitment is planned or made: target is 100% renewable electricity by [target year, no later than 2050]. Assess geographic procurement complexity — instruments must be procured market-by-market.

## Step 4: Circular Economy Strategy

**Lead**: Circular Economy Strategist

Apply the 10-R hierarchy to material topics identified in Phase 2:

| R-Level | Strategy | Application |
|---|---|---|
| R0: Refuse | Eliminate product/material | Phase out single-use plastics; refuse non-recyclable inputs |
| R1: Rethink | Business model change | Product-as-a-service; sharing models |
| R2: Reduce | Reduce material use | Lightweighting; design optimization; dematerialization |
| R3: Reuse | Reuse as-is | Take-back and reuse programs; reusable packaging |
| R4: Repair | Repair and maintain | Repair service; design for repairability |
| R5: Refurbish | Restore and update | Refurbishment program; remanufacturing |
| R6: Remanufacture | Use parts in new product | Component recovery; remanufacturing stream |
| R7: Repurpose | Use for different function | Upcycling; application repurposing |
| R8: Recycle | Process for raw material | Recyclable design; post-consumer recycled content target |
| R9: Recover | Energy recovery | Waste-to-energy for true residuals only (last resort) |

Design-for-circularity principles: ease of disassembly, material labeling for recycling, avoiding material combinations that prevent recyclability, extended product life.

Material Circularity Indicator (Ellen MacArthur MCI): quantify current circularity; set improvement target.

## Step 5: Carbon Market Strategy

**Lead**: Carbon Markets Analyst

### Role of carbon credits in net zero strategy

**What credits can do**: offset residual emissions that cannot be eliminated through operational changes; enable VCMI claims if SBTi target is set and being met; support near-term climate finance while decarbonization ramps up

**What credits cannot do** (per SBTi, VCMI, IPCC):
- Substitute for emissions reductions in the near-term target period
- Be used to claim net zero without ≥90% absolute reduction
- Be used to create "carbon neutral" product claims without full scope definition, verification, and ISO 14068 compliance

### Credit quality standards

For any credits used in public claims, require:
- **ICVCM Core Carbon Principles (CCPs)**: additionality, permanence, MRV, co-benefits, no double counting
- **Verra VCS + CCB** (for forestry/land use) or **Gold Standard** (for renewable energy / community projects)
- **Vintage**: recent vintage preferred; avoid old credits with questionable additionality
- **Avoided emissions vs. removal**: prioritize removal credits (BECCS, DACCS, biochar, soil carbon, blue carbon) for long-term net zero claims; avoid substituting cheap avoidance credits for reductions

### VCMI claims pathway

VCMI Claims Code (2023) requires:
1. SBTi-validated near-term target (1.5°C aligned) — OR credible equivalent
2. Making credible progress toward target
3. Purchase and retirement of high-quality carbon credits (ICVCM CCP label preferred)
4. Claim tier: Silver (≥60% Scope 3 value), Gold (≥100% Scope 3 value), Platinum (100% Scope 3 + removal focus)
